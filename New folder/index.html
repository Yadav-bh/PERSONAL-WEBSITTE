Commercial Banks Analysis

🔑 1. Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)

Meaning: यो ratio ले बैंकले आफनो ऋण तथा जोखिमलाई धान्न कति पूँजी छ भन्ने देखाउँछ।

Why Important: Strong CAR (>11%) = safe and well-managed bank.

Regulator's Minimum: NRB requires minimum 11% Total Capital Adequacy Ratio.

🔑 2. Net Profit / Earnings Growth

Meaning: बैंकले हरेक वर्ष कति नाफा कमाइरहेको छ।

Why Important: लगातार बढ्दो नाफा हुने बैंकमा लगानी सुरक्षित र लाभदायक हुन्छ।

🔑 3. Non-Performing Loans (NPL) Ratio

Meaning: बैंकले दिएको कर्जा मध्ये कति फिर्ता नआएको छ भन्ने सूचक।

Ideal Level: Less than 2% is good.

Higher NPL = Risk: अधिक NPL भएको बैंक असुरक्षित मानिन्छ।

🔑 4. Loan to Deposit Ratio (LDR)

Meaning: जम्मा निक्षेपको कति प्रतिशत कर्जा दिइएको छ।

Healthy Range: Around 80–90%.

Too High = Liquidity Risk

🔑 5. Cost of Fund vs. Return on Loan

Meaning: बैंकले कति ब्याजमा निक्षेप संकलन गर्छ र कति ब्याजमा कर्जा दिन्छ?

Net Interest Margin (NIM): High NIM is good — it means better profit from interest spread.

🔑 6. Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Meaning: प्रति सेयर कति नाफा कमायो भन्ने कुरा।

Benchmark: EPS > 20 = strong profitability.

🔑 7. Dividend History (Bonus + Cash)

Meaning: कम्पनीले अघिल्लो वर्षहरूमा कस्तो लाभांश दिएको थियो?

Why Important: Consistent dividend-paying banks are stable and trusted by investors.

🔑 8. Reserve and Surplus

Meaning: कम्पनीले कति जगेडा राखेको छ?

High Reserves = Potential for Bonus Shares.

🔑 9. Branch Network and Digital Reach

Wider branch and mobile banking reach = More customer base = More deposits and loans.

🔑 10. Regulatory Compliance & NRB Policies

Meaning: बैंकले NRB को सबै नियम पालना गरेको छ कि छैन?

Why Important: NRB penalties can reduce profit and trust.

🔑 11. Management and Governance

Check: CEO, board of directors, ownership structure — are they experienced and transparent?

Governance is key in long-term value creation.

🔑 12. Market Capitalization & Valuation (P/E Ratio, P/B Ratio)

Compare: Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio with sector average.

Low P/E = undervalued (potential to grow)

High P/E = possibly overvalued

Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio: Under 1.5 is usually good for banks.

Bonus: 🧠 Special Factors in NEPSE Context

  • Merger & Acquisition News: Boosts short-term stock price.
  • Monetary Policy & NRB Circulars: Directly impact banking liquidity, dividend, loan-deposit limits.
  • Interest Rate Trend: High interest = lower lending = low profit (short-term)
  • Macroeconomic Factors: Remittance, inflation, GDP, etc.
